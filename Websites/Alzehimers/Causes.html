Causes

Alzheimers is a disease that usually affects people 65 and older. Some Nicknames that are given to this disease are Ollie, and Old Timers Disease.


How does Alzheimer's enter the body?


Alzheimer's is created when an unusual amount of protein
builds up around the brain cells. There are two proteins that are called,
amyloid and tau. Amyloid creates plaques
around the cell which disturbs the cell's functions. Tau makes tangles in the brain.

How does Alzheimer's attack the organs?


Alzheimer's causes important organs to shut down.
This causes people to lose their ability to move 
and do things that people would normally do.
For example, eating, drinking, etc. As time goes on
they lose their ability to do everything. They can’t walk, talk, or move.



Where in the world does Alzheimer's strike?


According to google, Alzheimer's is most common in Western
Europe and a little bit of North America, but anyone can get it.
People who are 65 years or older are usually affected with this disease. 





Why does Alzheimer's mostly affect this part of the world?

Alzheimer's usually affects people that don't have much contact with bacteria
or other viruses because those peoples immune system would not develop. This can lead to dementia.
This might mean that Western Europe may not have contact with as many viruses or br bacteria
other places/countries have.
