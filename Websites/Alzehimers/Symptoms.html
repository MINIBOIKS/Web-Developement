Symptoms

Some symptoms of Alzheimers are, memory loss, poor judgment, losing track of dates or where they are,
taking longer to complete daily tasks.


What Organs does Alzheimers affect?

Alzheimer's first destroys neuron's and parts of the brain that are involved in
memory. This includes the entorhinal cortex and hippocampus. The entorhinal
cortex and the and hippocampus are part of the brains memory. The entorhinal
cortex is like a gateway to the hippocampus.


Does Alzheimers cause Organ failure?

Yes, it does. In fact, it causes many organ failures. Because of this they can get
heart attacks, Dehydration and malnutrition. They would even forget how they eat
or drink food and water.


How are the organs Attacked?

The brain is the most important in your body. Without it you can't do anything.
Today we are walking, talking, eating, and drinking normally because our brain
is functioning well. The elders that have Alzheimer's don't have a healthy brain
like us. Their brain shrinks. When their brain shrinks, the places where the
memory is stored also shrinks. This makes them forget how to do things. For
example walking, you can't just get up and walk without thinking about
anything. Your brain tells you haw to walk. So, if the brain isn't. able
to do anything then the organs can't either which causes them to stop working.




Do the symptoms change as the disease progresses?

They don't change but, more symptoms are added. They have difficulty
swallowing, walking, and they become exposed to infections. One of
the main ones is pneumonia.
